Chicken Curry (murgi tarkhari)

Do you want to learn how to make a traditional, rustic, authentic Chicken Curry from your dusty run-down council estate kitchen?

Say no more.

Now don't be offended by my remarks on your finances or state of your kitchen, because it is actually surprisingly relevant. Traditionally, these curries are made in thin, metal pots on top of coal and burning wood, which isn't too far off from what the Hillingdon, Ealing or Tower Hamlets councils provide - which is great!

This spicy chicken curry is a popular favourite amongst all the coconut-bengalis scattered around Europe, America, Canada and wherever else their parents may have ran off to. Now, if you too are frightened by fish, and can't fathom holding a pescatarian diet - let's begin with your ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole baby chicken cut into curry pieces or 1 kg of boneless chicken.
  • 2 large onions
  • 5 green cardomom seeds
  • 5 long cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 1 dried bayleaf
  • 2 Tbsp chilli powder
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp Tumeric powder
  • 1 Tbsp Cumin powder
  • 1/2 Tbsp Coriander seed powder

Equipment:

  • 1 medium sized spoon (preferably wood)
  • 1 big pot (including lid)
  • 1 cereal bowl
  • 1 mini plate
  • 1 cutting board
  • 1 large sharp knife
